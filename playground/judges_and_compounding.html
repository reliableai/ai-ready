Judges & Compounding Lab

Cross-cutting layer + Capstone — "Every uncertainty applies again at the judge level."
What is this playground about? (click to expand)

Sources #1–9 assumed a perfect judge. You asked "is this response correct?" and got the right answer. In reality, the judge is itself an imperfect system. Every uncertainty source applies again at the judge level.

Compounding: Judge uncertainty doesn't just add to other uncertainties — it multiplies. A noisy judge + sampling noise = much wider CIs than either alone. A biased judge + biased sample = errors that reinforce each other.

Part 1 — Judge Noise: Same judge, same input, different scores each time. See how judge noise widens your confidence intervals.

Part 2 — Judge Bias: A generous vs harsh judge. Same outputs, different scorecards.

Part 3 — Compounding: Stack multiple sources together and see the total uncertainty explode.

Tabs: Judge Noise = same judge, random variation per item. Judge Bias = generous vs harsh judge on same outputs. Compounding = stack multiple sources (sampling noise, judge noise, judge bias, sample bias) and watch the total uncertainty multiply.
