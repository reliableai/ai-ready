SLIDE STRUCTURE & CONTENT
================================================================================

OPENING (Slides 1-3)
  1. Title Slide: "Sources of Uncertainty in AI Evaluation"
  2. The Setup: Unified scenario with 4-metric scorecard (colored bars)
  3. Overview: Nine sources + judge layer

SOURCE 1: SAMPLING NOISE (Slides 4-7)
  4. Title card (#1 - blue accent)
  5. The Problem: Small n, big wobbles
  6. The Fix: Confidence intervals, beta posteriors, bootstrap
  7. Does More Data Help? YES - uncertainty collapses as √(1/n)

SOURCE 2: SAMPLING BIAS (Slides 8-11)
  8. Title card (#2 - orange accent)
  9. The Problem: Wrong population mix
  10. The Fix: Know production, stratified sampling, per-segment reporting
  11. Does More Data Help? NO - more biased data is still biased

SOURCE 3: OVERFITTING (Slides 12-15)
  12. Title card (#3 - green accent)
  13. The Problem: Information leakage, data shapes system
  14. The Lab: Judge calibration & structured extraction tasks
  15. Does More Data Help? PARTIALLY - process discipline is key

SOURCE 4: MULTIPLE HYPOTHESIS TESTING (Slides 16-20)
  16. Title card (#4 - purple accent)
  17. The Problem: Selection bias, best-of-N has inflated score
  18. Key Insights: Bias scales with N, more search = more fooling yourself
  19. The Fix: Two-stage evaluation, Bonferroni correction
  20. Does More Data Help? PARTIALLY - real fix is process

SOURCE 5: VARIANCE ACROSS DOMAINS (Slides 21-24)
  21. Title card (#5 - blue accent)
  22. The Problem: Simpson's paradox, aggregate hides real variance
  23. The Fix: Per-segment reporting, SLAs, adaptive systems
  24. Does More Data Help? NO - variance is property of world

SOURCE 6: CHOICE OF METRIC (Slides 25-27)
  25. Title card (#6 - orange accent)
  26. The Problem: Same data, different winners with different metrics
  27. The Fix: Pre-commit to metric, metric hierarchy, design decision

SOURCE 7: TEMPORAL DRIFT (Slides 28-30)
  28. Title card (#7 - green accent)
  29. The Problem: World changes, your January eval ≠ April reality
  30. The Fix: Continuous monitoring, drift detection, version everything

SOURCE 8: PROMPT BRITTLENESS (Slides 31-34)
  31. Title card (#8 - purple accent)
  32. Real Examples: Reordering (54%→93%), punctuation (9.4%→54.9%), etc.
  33. The Fix: Sensitivity testing, report ranges, robustness as criterion
  34. Does More Data Help? NO - brittleness is model property

SOURCE 9: LLM EVOLUTION (Slides 35-37)
  35. Title card (#9 - blue accent)
  36. The Problem: Chen et al. example (GPT-4: 97.6% → 2.4%)
  37. The Fix: Version pinning, regression tests, treat updates as deployments

THE JUDGE LAYER (Slides 38-41)
  38. Title card: The Judge Layer (red accent with scales icon)
  39. The Multiplier: Every source applies again at judge level
  40. Compounding Effect: Perfect judge CI ±0.2 → add judge noise ±0.4
  41. Subjectivity: Experts disagree on "what is a 4?"

CAPSTONE: COMPOUNDING (Slides 42-46)
  42. Title card: "Uncertainty Compounds" (yellow warning accent)
  43. Stacking Table: Watch CI grow and shift with each source
  44. Anti-Intuitions: What students need to unlearn
  45. The Key Takeaway: Scorecard conditions on judge, prompt, data, moment
  46. Does More Data Help? Summary for all 9 sources

CLOSING (Slides 47-48)
  47. Interactive Tools: Links to playgrounds and notebooks
  48. Final Quote: Tukey, Box, Ioannidis wisdom
