Designing Experiments and Mastering Uncertainty — Part II

Estimators, the Central Limit Theorem, and bootstrapping

Guiding principles

"Any measurement, without knowledge of the uncertainty, is meaningless." — Walter Lewin, MIT

"Those who are able to represent what they do not know make better decisions." — Chris Piech, CS109 class at Stanford

TOPICS

The Series (continued)

4 — Random Variables for Random Variables

Distributions of estimators. The Beta distribution as a tool for modeling uncertainty about probabilities. How to assess an AI system and reason about the uncertainty in your measurements. Updating beliefs with data and credible intervals.

5 — Adding Random Variables

Combining independent random variables. The Central Limit Theorem and why sums of random variables tend toward the normal distribution. Practical consequences for AI evaluation.

6 — Bootstrapping

Bootstrapping methods for practical uncertainty estimation. Building sampling distributions from a single dataset, computing confidence intervals, and comparing systems without parametric assumptions.
